The Big Questions

Get to know me metaphysically

Who am I?

My name is Gabriel San Martín and I am a freelance translator who works from Japanese or English into European Spanish and specializes in video games. I have participated in projects such as Ni no Kuni II and several titles of the Pokémon franchise. I have enjoyed video games from an early age, when my father bought, I mean, gifted a Nintendo 64 to me for Christmas. In recent years, I have acquired experience in the different stages of the localization of games for consoles, PC and mobile devices, and have also dipped a toe in teaching that whole process. My work experience and my personal preferences have always been inclined towards role playing games, but both as a fan and a professional I make sure to broaden my horizons and learn about all sorts of genres regularly. That is one of the biggest privileges of this line of work.

Besides my main languages, I use the rest of FIGS languages (when their texts are available) as a reference during projects to ensure a top quality translation. I studied French for six years in high school, one year of German as part of my undergrad studies plus a one-week-long course at Goethe-Institut, and I am learning Italian among other languages by myself (and by eavesdropping some of my neighbouring colleagues conversations when there was such a thing as an office/back when I worked in an office).

I have been an avid reader all my life and, like most translation students, I longed to translate literature. If you work in editorial translation and would like to give me a chance to fulfill that dream, my Contact section is wide open. And so are my interests. I foster a curiosity towards an array of topics and media, so any translation or localization project that I am suitable for is very welcome. You can take a look at a brief summary of my experience or download the full PDF version of my résumé in the CV section above.

If you are wondering why I talk about gender and LGBTQ+ issues, the reason is that I belong to a couple of those letters, I was a founding member of an association for LGBTQI+ rights, and gender has been a life-long personal dilemma, which I am constantly trying to solve. I even dedicated my masters dissertation to it. In my work, I always apply a questioning approach and stand up for equality. Lately, I have taught myself and others about accessibility and the ability of the localization team to support good practices in video games so that everyone can enjoy video games equally.

Where do I come from and where am I going?




I was born in Asturias, in Northwestern Spain, and I set out to explore the world as soon as I could. I studied in Catalonia, Ireland and Japan. I took several languages as mementos from each of them, and a masters degree in Audiovisual Translation to boot. I have lived in the United Kingdom, and I currently reside in Canada (EDT). I do not know what my future destination is.
